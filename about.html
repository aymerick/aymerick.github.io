I'm Aymerick, a software developer who lives near Rennes, France.

I work remotely since 2008 as a full stack web developer at Fotonauts, a company founded by a group of ex-Apple engineers to create fotopedia, a photo community website.

My current centers of interest are the Go language, the Ember.js framework and home automation.

Fotopedia

Since 2008

I started at fotonauts as a DevOp, setuping servers with Puppet then Chef. I then developed several features:

  • Photo upload - It started as a Merb application, then became a generic (private) gem used by both the main Rails application and by Hadoop scripts for batch photo processing.
  • Async job infra - The fotopedia app is highly asynchronous. We use Resque with a MongoDB database to run asynchronous jobs.
  • News feed - After several iterations, including a custom actors architecture powered by EventMachine, I finally built the Activr gem that currently manages the activity feeds on fotopedia. Activr needs a MongoDB database, and it automatically hook up with Rails and Resque.
  • Reputation - I developed a system a la Stack Overflow to attribute points to fotopedia users when they perform good actions. It permits to detect valuable contributors, to motivate users and it helps our internal curation team on their daily work by preventing trollers actions.
  • Achievements - Yeah! Gamification! Do stuff to earn badges :)
  • Notifications / Newsletter - Mail and web notifications, publishing of activities on facebook and twitter, and a custom newsletter system.

So, even I have mainly done backend stuff, I acquired experience in frontend development too:

  • I'm maybe not a CSS gran masta, but I do know enough to feel confortable with it, and I know SASS. Moreover, at fotonauts we do responsive design, even for emails, yeah, thank you Ink.
  • I participated too in the development of the Reporter section of the site, built thanks to a custom Javascript framework created by our frontend team and that have, among others great features, an object layer similar to Ember.js one.

The fotonauts team is based in Paris. Most of the time I work from my home at Betton, but one day a week I go to Paris to work with my great colleagues (and to drink some beers with them).

Apple contractor

2004 - 2007

I met Fotonauts founders while working as a contractor for Apple. During that mission I implemented the SyncML protocol in the iSync synchronisation tool shipped with MacOS X.

The addition of the SyncML protocol into iSync permitted to add support for tens of new mobile devices for Mac users.

I worked remotely from a contractor company in Rennes, reporting to the Apple staff located in Paris.

Telecom industry
